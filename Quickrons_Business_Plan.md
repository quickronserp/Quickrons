## 1. Executive Summary

Quickrons is a hyper-local food platform that does what Zomato and Swiggy cannot: it curates a **premium tier of authentic, high-quality food** prepared by **vetted home makers, premium hotels, and professional caterers**, delivered by a **trained, branded rider fleet**, with **Forra Foods** as the anchor flagship kitchen.

Indian users — especially urban professionals, young families, NRIs, and elderly customers — are increasingly tired of cloud-kitchen sameness, MSG-heavy "restaurant" food, and unreliable delivery quality. Quickrons solves this by integrating four supply-side parties (home makers, hotels, caterers, riders) into a single, trust-first marketplace, with Forra Foods producing high-margin signature SKUs as the base economic engine.

We will earn revenue from **four streams**: (1) commission on premium partner orders (15–22%), (2) direct margin on Forra Foods SKUs (35–45%), (3) subscription "Quickrons Plus" (₹149/month), and (4) catering booking fees (5%).

We will launch in one Bengaluru zone (Indiranagar–Koramangala–HSR) with 25 partners, 60 riders, and a 2,000-order/day target by month 6. Seed ask: **₹3.5 Cr** for 18 months of runway.

## 2. The Problem

Indian food-delivery consumers face a paradox: the apps offer thousands of options, yet finding **honest, home-style, freshly cooked food** is harder than ever.

The pain points we keep hearing in user interviews:

The food on Zomato/Swiggy is often from anonymous cloud kitchens with no quality accountability. Restaurants reuse oil, over-salt, and rely on packaged masalas. There is no easy way to order genuine, home-cooked Andhra, Kerala, Bengali, or Hyderabadi food prepared by someone's *amma* or *paati* — yet that is precisely what working professionals miss most. Hotel-grade banquet food is gated behind ₹1,500 minimums and full-day catering bookings, making it inaccessible for a normal family dinner. Riders are squeezed by aggregators, unbranded, and often poorly trained, hurting last-mile experience. And caterers — who run real, hygienic kitchens — are stuck on WhatsApp and have no digital storefront for daily orders.

Every existing aggregator has chased volume. Nobody has chased **trust**.

## 3. The Solution: Quickrons

Quickrons is a single mobile app that integrates five parties under one trust-graded marketplace:

**1. Forra Foods (Flagship Kitchen)** — Our own brand. Daily-fresh thalis, biryanis, healthy boxes, breakfast tiffins. High-margin, recipe-controlled, our own quality bar. Operates as the price floor and the "always-available" option.

**2. Authentic Home Makers** — Vetted, FSSAI-licensed home cooks who prepare regional specialties in limited daily quantity. Onboarded through home-kitchen audit, food-safety training, and a closed Quickrons academy.

**3. Premium Hotels** — 4★ and 5★ hotel kitchens (Taj, ITC, Marriott franchises, Sheraton, regional premium chains). Offered a *small-order* SKU rail (single thalis, single biryanis, premium snack boxes) at ₹399–₹999 — a price point hotels cannot service through their own room-service-grade systems.

**4. Caterers** — Daily tiffin services, occasion caterers, and specialty caterers (vegan, Jain, diabetic, keto). Get both retail orders and bulk bookings.

**5. Branded Quickrons Riders** — Trained, uniformed, insulated-bag-equipped riders with a tablet-based hand-off protocol for premium orders. Riders earn 18–25% above market through tips-baked-in pricing.

The user opens the app, sees a clean two-tab home screen — **"Quick Today"** (anything-in-30-min) and **"Curated"** (premium pre-orderable from home makers and hotels) — and orders with confidence.

## 4. Unique Value Proposition

While Zomato and Swiggy compete on speed and breadth, Quickrons competes on three things they cannot replicate without breaking their own model:

**Trust at the source.** Every partner is vetted, every kitchen is inspected, every rider is trained. Every dish has a "Made by" card with a real face, real story, and real FSSAI ID.

**Authenticity over volume.** We deliberately cap partner counts per cuisine per zone to protect demand and quality. No "everything-everywhere" — just "the right dish from the right kitchen."

**A premium tier that actually exists.** Hotels and caterers on Quickrons offer SKUs you cannot find on existing aggregators, because we built the partnership and the pricing model from scratch around small-batch, scheduled delivery.

The platform integrates all five parties — riders, home makers, hotels, caterers, and Forra Foods — and earns commission across each. Every order is a multi-sided transaction, and every party benefits.

## 5. Market Opportunity

The Indian online food-delivery market crossed **₹1,15,000 Cr GMV in FY25** and is projected to compound at 16–18% through 2030. Premium and "home-made authentic" is the fastest-growing micro-segment, growing at 30%+ annually but still under 6% of total GMV — meaning the prize is large, growing, and structurally underserved.

Bengaluru alone has 1.4 crore residents, of whom roughly 22 lakh are working professionals in the ₹15–₹50 LPA bracket — our prime ICP. They order food 11–14 times a month on average, and 38% in our user research said they would pay 20–30% more for "food I trust."

A single-zone launch (Indiranagar + Koramangala + HSR Layout) covers ~6 lakh residents and 90,000+ ICP households. Capturing 4% of their food-spend translates to ~₹4.2 Cr monthly GMV inside one zone — a credible 18-month target.

## 6. Platform Segments — How Each Party Wins

**Forra Foods (flagship)** anchors the platform. By owning a vertical kitchen, we guarantee SLA, control unit economics, and get a daily training ground for our ops team. Forra signature SKUs (biryani, thalis, breakfast tiffins, healthy boxes) carry 35–45% gross margin and serve as our customer-acquisition wedge.

**Home makers** earn ₹18,000–₹55,000 per month from a dignified home-based business. We handle packaging, pickup, and payments. They cook 2–3 SKUs per day, in limited quantities, and sell out by lunch. We provide one-time kitchen-setup support, FSSAI assistance, and ingredient-sourcing partnerships.

**Premium hotels** unlock a new revenue line for their idle off-peak kitchens. Most hotel F&B operates 30–55% utilization between 3pm–7pm and after 10pm. Quickrons gives them a high-margin small-order channel without the cost of running their own delivery.

**Caterers** get a daily-orders pipeline (subscription tiffins) plus a discoverable storefront for occasion bookings. We bring them weddings, office events, and birthday parties they could not access through WhatsApp alone.

**Riders** join a branded fleet with insurance, weather-gear, performance bonuses, and dignity. We pay 18–25% above prevailing aggregator rates because our average order value is 2.4x higher.

## 7. Business Model and Commission Structure

Quickrons earns from four interconnected streams.

**Stream 1 — Partner Commissions.** Home makers pay 12% (kept low to preserve their margin and grow the supply base). Caterers pay 15%. Premium hotels pay 18–22% depending on AOV slab. This sits in the same band as Zomato/Swiggy take-rate, but we offer hotels and caterers a digital channel they previously did not have.

**Stream 2 — Forra Foods Direct Margin.** Every Forra order earns us 35–45% gross. Forra is roughly 30% of total order volume in year one and is the structural profit lever.

**Stream 3 — Quickrons Plus Subscription.** ₹149/month for free delivery, priority slots, and 8% off Forra orders. Targeted ARPU lift of ₹400/month per subscriber.

**Stream 4 — Catering Booking Fees.** 5% on bookings over ₹5,000, paid by the customer transparently as a "platform service fee."

A representative ₹600 home-maker order: ₹600 GMV → ₹72 platform commission (12%) + ₹40 rider fee charged to user → Net contribution ~₹52 after rider payout and packaging. A representative ₹420 Forra order: ₹420 GMV → ₹160 gross margin → Net contribution ~₹95 after rider payout and packaging.

Blended contribution margin at month 18 target: **₹38 per order**.

## 9. Go-to-Market and Marketing Strategy

The marketing wedge is content, not coupons. We will launch a video series — "Cooked by Hand" — featuring our home-maker partners in their kitchens, telling the story of one signature dish. Each episode is 90 seconds, distributed on Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and as in-app first-screen content. The same content doubles as partner trust collateral.

Performance marketing will run on Instagram, Google, and Meta, but the primary acquisition engine in months 1–6 will be **referral economics**: ₹150 credit for each user who refers a friend, both sides, redeemable on Forra Foods only. This compounds GMV directly into our highest-margin SKU.

Offline, we will partner with corporate HR teams in Indiranagar and Koramangala (Razorpay, Swiggy itself, Flipkart, Cred, ShareChat, Zerodha) to seed Quickrons Plus subscriptions as an employee-benefit add-on. A single 500-employee office hooked into Quickrons Plus produces ₹74,500 of monthly recurring subscription revenue alone.

## 10. Operations and Quality Control

Quality is the product. Our operations rest on four protocols.

**Partner Onboarding Protocol.** Every home maker, hotel, and caterer goes through a 7-day vetting: kitchen visit, FSSAI verification, cooking test, packaging test, and a customer-blind taste panel of three Quickrons staff. Pass rate is targeted at 60% — we are deliberately selective.

**Continuous Quality Monitoring.** Every order asks the customer for a 3-tap rating on temperature, taste, and packaging. Any partner whose 7-day rolling rating drops below 4.2/5 enters a 14-day improvement plan, after which they may be paused.

**Rider Protocol.** All premium orders use an insulated tamper-evident bag. The rider scans the seal at handoff. Customers can rate the rider's professionalism separately from the food.

**Cold-Chain and SLA.** Forra and hotel orders use temperature-monitored bags. SLA is 35 minutes for "Quick Today" and ±10 minutes for "Curated" pre-orders.

## 11. Technology Architecture

Mobile apps for iOS and Android are built in **React Native (Expo)** for fast cross-platform iteration. Backend on Node.js + PostgreSQL + Redis, hosted on AWS Mumbai region. Real-time order/rider tracking via Socket.io. Payments via Razorpay (UPI, cards, wallets). Maps via Mapbox (cheaper than Google at scale).

We are starting with the Expo MVP shipped alongside this plan — see `QuickronsApp/` in the workspace. The MVP covers user authentication, browsing across all five segments, cart, checkout (mock), partner onboarding, and a rider view.

The platform is designed multi-role from day one: a single user account can be a customer, a rider, and a partner — switching profiles within the app — which dramatically reduces user-acquisition friction.

## 14. Risks and Mitigation

The dominant risk is **supply quality at scale** — the model breaks if home-maker quality drifts. Mitigation: hard cap of partners per zone, 90-day rolling audits, and ratings-based pause/remove protocol.

Second is **aggregator counter-attack** — Zomato/Swiggy could launch a "premium" tab. Mitigation: lock in exclusive home-maker partners with non-compete onboarding and Forra Foods as a structural moat that aggregators cannot replicate (they will not run their own kitchens at scale).

Third is **rider unit economics at low volume** — we mitigate with higher AOV (averaging ₹520 vs Zomato/Swiggy ₹280) and a smaller initial fleet sized to demand.

Fourth is **regulatory** — FSSAI compliance for home kitchens is the operational gating item. Mitigation: in-house FSSAI assistance team that handles licenses for partners as part of onboarding.
